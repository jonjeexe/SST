🎯 Exam Strategy & Study Plan

Toppers' tips, revision schedule & how to score 90+

12-Week Revision Plan

Weeks 1–2

History — Complete Syllabus

Finish all 5 chapters. Make one-pager notes. Memorize key dates, events, names of movements and leaders.

Weeks 3–4

Geography — All Chapters

Map practice daily. Learn resources, agriculture, industries and their locations. Revise data tables.

Weeks 5–6

Political Science

Cover all 8 chapters. Focus on definitions, examples, federalism, democracy concepts. Make comparison tables.

Weeks 7–8

Economics

Learn all definitions thoroughly. Practice data interpretation. Revise SHG, MNC, globalisation concepts.

Weeks 9–10

Full Revision Round 1

Quick revision of all 4 subjects. Attempt previous year papers (2019–2024). Identify weak chapters.

Weeks 11–12

Mock Tests + Final Revision

Full mock tests under exam conditions. Revise important questions, map points, and source-based passages.

Subject-Wise Scoring Tips

📜 History Tips

  • Learn names of leaders, dates & treaties for each chapter
  • Practice source-based questions — these are 4 marks each
  • Write points in paragraphs, not bullets for long answers
  • Use sub-headings in 5-mark answers
  • Memorize Gandhiji's movements: Non-Cooperation, CDM, Quit India
  • Learn the role of print, women, peasants, tribes separately

🌍 Geography Tips

  • Map work = easy 5 marks — practice every day!
  • Learn states for each resource, crop, industry on blank map
  • Memorize exact definitions: soil types, forest types, crop seasons
  • Learn Kharif, Rabi, Zaid crops with examples
  • Water harvesting traditional methods by state
  • Road types, national highways, major ports — very common

⚖️ Political Science Tips

  • Learn examples from India + world for every concept
  • Federalism: compare India with Belgium & Sri Lanka
  • Democracy definitions + advantages + limitations
  • Political parties: types, functions, challenges
  • Gender, religion, caste: how they affect politics
  • Make a table: Outcomes of Democracy

💰 Economics Tips

  • Learn all bold terms: GDP, HDI, SHG, MNC, etc.
  • Practice table/data interpretation questions
  • Globalisation pros & cons — very common long answer
  • Sectors of economy: comparison table is a must
  • Credit, loans, banks, formal vs informal sector
  • Money and trade: barter system → modern money evolution

📝 General Exam Tips

  • Read every question carefully — underline keywords
  • Start with your strongest subject to build confidence
  • Allocate time: ~20 min per section + 10 min review
  • MCQs first — don't spend more than 30 sec each
  • Use neat diagrams, headings and point format
  • Write neatly — presentation adds perceived value

📚 Resources to Use

  • NCERT textbook — this is your Bible, read it fully
  • NCERT Exemplar for extra practice questions
  • Previous 5 years' CBSE board papers (compulsory!)
  • CBSE sample paper released for current year
  • Revision notes + mind maps from trusted sources
  • Do NOT rely only on guide books — NCERT first
